Wave

"wave" is a tactical media commentary on the stigma around mental illness and the barriers people face when seeking help. It is a conceptual app meant to connect a younger demographic of users and nearby therapists, exploiting the familiarity of dating service apps along with their associated casualness to reduce fear and intimidation as obstacles to seeking help.

It was a team project where the deliverable was a website of the intervention. Among my group of three, every member of the group contributed to the concept and logistics of the app. We then divided the work into three categories: website content (including text and statistics), graphics (such as logo and app screenshots), and web design, of which I took the last.

I used a Bootstrap template to create our live website, which can be found here.

Later, I wanted to expand on the project and prototype the interface. I used Adobe XD to design both versions of the app, one for the normal user (demo here) and the other for the therapist (demo here).

Someone seeking help would only have to download the app and create a profile, but a therapist wanting to join the community would have to apply on the website to create an account. Additionally, no user or therapist can search for another user; users will be able to see therapists that match their settings, preferences, and swipes, while therapists can only respond to messages they receive. This way, users can maintain their privacy and a sense of control over their interactions on the app.
